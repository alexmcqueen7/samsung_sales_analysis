# Samsung Sales Analysis

## Description
This project uses a Samsung Global Product Sales Dataset, which simulates a realistic global sales ledger for Samsung Electronics products. The data includes four years of global Samsung product sales across 52 countries. The project uses Google BigQuery to analyze the data and Power BI to create an executive summary dashboard showcasing key data and findings from the analysis. The goal of this project was answer a set of self-determined business questions based off the available data. The findings do not reflect the real-world state of Samsung Electronics sales.

## Questions Answered
1. Which regions and countries generate the most revenue, and how is this changing over time?
2. Which products and product categories are driving the highest revenue?
3. How do discounts affect revenue and units sold across regions and product categories?
4. Which sales channels and customer segments are most profitable, and how do demographics affect purchasing behavior?
5. How is 5G adoption impacting sales revenue and units sold across regions?

## Results
1. Europe holds the highest share of Samsung's revenue between 2021 and 2024, with its share averaging at a little over a third of the global  total. This is closely followed by Asia, who's revenue and sales share sits at approximately 29% of the global total. Across the four year period shown in the data, there is a negligible change in the revenue/sales share of each region. The top 5 countries driving revenue are Greece, UAE, Austria, Norway, and France, respectively.
   
2. Due to its high Average Selling Price (ASP), premium Smart TV models are the strongest drivers of revenue, generating approximately $5.56M, making this the top-performing product category. Flagship smartphones also contribute significantly, with the Galaxy S line producing around $4.73M and the Galaxy Z foldable series generating about $2.77M. This indicates that high-end consumer electronics—particularly premium televisions and flagship mobile devices—play a central role in Samsung’s revenue performance. At the individual product level, several premium devices stand out as major contributors. The Samsung Neo QLED 8K QN900C generated the highest revenue at roughly $2.23M, followed by the Samsung OLED S95C 65-inch and other high-end television models. Foldable smartphones such as the Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 5 also rank among the top-performing products, highlighting strong demand for innovative and premium mobile technology within Samsung’s portfolio.

3. Discounts appear to have a moderate but not dramatic effect on purchasing behaviour across the dataset. Most transactions include relatively small discounts (typically under 20%), and the analysis shows that average units sold per transaction remain fairly consistent across discount ranges. However, the majority of total revenue is generated within the 0–5% discount range, suggesting that strong product demand allows many Samsung products to sell at minimal promotional reductions. Across regions and product categories, discount levels are also relatively similar, generally averaging around 5–6%. High-revenue categories such as Smart TVs and Galaxy S smartphones maintain strong sales despite modest discounting, while regions like Europe and Asia generate the highest total revenue without significantly higher discount rates. This indicates that revenue performance is driven more by product demand and market size than by aggressive discounting strategies.

4. Sales through official Samsung retail channels generate the highest overall revenue, with Samsung Stores and major e-commerce platforms performing particularly strongly. These channels benefit from both strong brand presence and direct customer engagement, helping drive consistent revenue across product categories. Business-focused channels such as Corporate/B2B sales also contribute significantly, indicating that enterprise purchases represent an important portion of Samsung’s total sales alongside consumer channels. In terms of customer demographics, the 25–34 age group generates the highest total revenue, suggesting that younger professionals are a key purchasing segment for Samsung products. Older consumers, particularly those aged 35–44 and 55+, also contribute substantial revenue, indicating strong demand across multiple age groups rather than a single dominant demographic. Overall, purchasing behaviour suggests that tech-savvy younger consumers drive high volumes of device purchases, while older demographics continue to support strong revenue through premium product adoption.

5. 5G-enabled products contribute a substantial share of Samsung’s total sales, generating approximately $8.5M in revenue from about 12,000 units sold in the dataset. While non-5G devices still account for a larger portion of total sales overall, the strong revenue generated by 5G products shows that next-generation connectivity is becoming an important driver of smartphone demand, particularly within premium device categories. Regionally, Asia and Europe show the strongest adoption of 5G devices, producing the highest revenue and unit sales for 5G-enabled products. These regions combine large consumer markets with faster adoption of advanced mobile technology. In contrast, regions such as Africa, South America, and the Middle East show lower but steadily growing 5G sales, suggesting that adoption is expanding as network infrastructure and device availability continue to improve.
